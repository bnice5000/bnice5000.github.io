    The Queen’s Mercy#

    “When the banners fall, every house fights alone.”

    Overview#

    The Queen’s Mercy is a fast, asymmetric, two-player, trick-taking card duel. Each player chooses a suit that contains 13 ranked cards plus one special card that functions as a Joker. Games are best of three rounds.

    • Players: 2
    • Cards: One suit of 14 cards per player (2 to Ace plus a Joker-equivalent)
    • Objective: Win tricks and score points to reach 10 in a round, then win two rounds to win the match

    Equipment and Deck Options#

    Each player needs one suit with 13 ranks plus a special card that behaves as the Joker. Here are some common setups:

    • Standard 52-Card Deck: Each player selects a suit (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣) and adds a Joker.
    • Heckadeck: Use the Beast (or 0) as the Joker-equivalent. Remove the other Beast (or 0) and the 11 from each suit so ranks become 2–10, J, Q, K, A, plus Beast (or 0).
    • French Deck with Chevalier: Use the Chevalier (Knight) as the Joker-equivalent. Your suit is 2–Ace including the Chevalier.

    Whichever deck you use, each player must end up with 14 cards: the 13 ranks and the suit’s Joker-equivalent.

    Setup#

    1. Each player takes one full suit (2–Ace) plus the chosen Joker/Beast/Chevalier for 14 total cards.
    2. Shuffle your suit and draw three cards.
    3. Keep your own draw and discard piles separate from your opponent’s.

    The Queen’s Cut — Choosing Suits and Opening Lead#

    Use this procedure to decide suits and who leads the first trick.

    1. Take any one full suit with its Joker-equivalent and shuffle it.
    2. One player cuts it into two face-down halves.
    3. The other player chooses a half and flips its top card.
    4. Higher rank wins the cut. Aces are high. Jokers beat face cards but lose to numbered cards. Ties are re-cut.
    5. The winner of the cut makes a royal choice:
      • Shape the Field: Choose which two suits will be used in this match, or
      • Claim a Suit: Choose which of the two selected suits you will play.
    6. The other player takes the remaining decision.
    7. Shuffle your suits, draw three, and begin.

    Order of Play (each trick)#

    1. Select and Reveal: Both players choose a card face down, then reveal.
    2. Determine Rank Winner: Higher rank wins. Aces are high.
      • Jokers are wild: a Joker loses to numbered cards and beats all face cards.
    3. Apply non-point effects that cancel, nullify, protect, or lock for this trick.
    4. Resolve point-changing powers using Power Resolution Order (see below).
    5. Apply base scoring: Trick winner gains +1 unless a card says otherwise. A tie gives no base point unless a card says otherwise.
    6. Discard the two played cards to their owners’ discard piles.
    7. Draw back to three cards if possible.

    Round End and Match Victory#

    • A round ends when all 14 cards have been played or when any player reaches 10 points.
    • The player with the higher total wins the round and takes one victory token.
    • Reset both scores to zero, reshuffle each suit, and play the next round.
    • The first player to two rounds wins the match.

    Scoring Rules#

    • Base point: winner of the trick gets +1, unless replaced or prevented.
    • Never Negative: a score cannot go below zero.
    • Steal only if possible: you can steal points only if your foe has that many points to lose.
    • Ties: a rank tie is neither a win nor a loss. Powers that require a win or loss do not trigger on a tie.

    Point Tally Tip: Use a shared pool of tokens. Take tokens when you gain points and return them when you lose points. Set aside one distinct token per round win.

    Key Terms#

    • Deck: your face-down draw pile.
    • Hand: the cards you currently hold.
    • Discard: your face-up pile of played cards.
    • Half Deck: your deck has seven or more cards.
    • Half Discard: your discard has seven or more cards.
    • Trick: one reveal and resolution of two cards.
    • Round: 14 tricks in which both suits are fully played.
    • Game / Match: best of three rounds.
    • Trick Lead: the player whose powers resolve first when ranks are equal. The loser of the previous trick is the lead. In round one, the lead is the player who chose the suits during the Queen’s Cut.
    • Steal: you gain the amount only if the foe can lose it.
    • Nullify: the specified card’s power does not trigger.
    • Locked: a locked score cannot change until the lock ends.
    • Advantage / Disadvantage: you are ahead or behind in points.
    • In Play: the two revealed cards of the current trick.

    Power Resolution Order#

    When both cards have powers that trigger in the same phase:

    1. Nullify effects resolve first.
    2. Then resolve in ascending rank order: Jack, Queen, King, Ace, Joker.
    3. If both cards share the same rank, the Trick Lead resolves first.
    4. Any effect that says “next trick” waits until after all scoring for the current trick.

    Card Powers by Suit#

    ♠ Spades - Power and Pressure#

    Card Power Name
    A♠ If you lose, steal 1 if possible. Power Grab
    K♠ If you win, +1. If you are not ahead after scoring, -1. Heavy Is the Crown
    Q♠ At end of scoring, if you have advantage, +1. If your foe has disadvantage, they +2. Queen’s Mercy
    J♠ If foe played a face this trick, foe -1. Intimidate
    Joker♠ If you win, foe chooses: you +1 or they -1. Ultimatum

    ♥ Hearts - Growth and Resilience#

    Card Power Name
    A♥ If you lose, +1. If your foe is at Half Discard, +2 instead. Second Wind
    K♥ If you lose, steal 1 if possible. Resolve
    Q♥ If you lose, lock foe’s score. Shield of Compassion
    J♥ If you win or tie, +1. Triumph
    Joker♥ +2. If you win, +1 more. Greater Restoration

    ♦ Diamonds - Wealth and Risk#

    Card Power Name
    A♦ If you win, +1. If you lose, -1. If you tie, +3. Double or Nothing
    K♦ If you win, steal 1 if possible. Hostile Takeover
    Q♦ If you have Advantage before scoring, +1. If you win, +1. Momentum Trade
    J♦ If you lose, foe -1. Short Sell
    Joker♦ Next trick: both players play a random card from the foe’s hand. Each player presents a hand, and the foe chooses which card to play. Market Chaos

    ♣ Clubs - Balance and Control#

    Card Power Name
    A♣ If you lose or tie, +1. If your foe is at Half Deck, +2 instead. Equilibrium
    K♣ If you win or tie, +2. If your foe has Disadvantage, they +1. Redistribution
    Q♣ If you win or tie, lock foe’s score. If you lose, +1. Stasis
    J♣ If foe played a face this trick, nullify that power. If the trick ties, +1. Counterpoise
    Joker♣ Both scores become locked after discarding the played cards. Return to Balance

    Example Trick#

    Setup

    • Brian plays Spades. Sarah plays Hearts.
    • Score before the trick: Sarah 5, Brian 4 (Sarah has Advantage).
    • Each player has three cards in hand and four in discard.
    • Brian is the Trick Lead.

    Reveal

    Brian: Q♠ (Queen’s Mercy)

    Sarah: A♥ (Second Wind)

    1. Rank Winner — Ace beats Queen, so Sarah wins.
    2. Non-point effects — none.
    3. Powers (ascending rank).
      • Q♠ checks Advantage. Brian does not have it, and Sarah is not at Disadvantage, so no effect.
      • A♥ triggers on loss. Sarah won, so no effect.
    4. Base scoring — winner gets +1. New totals: Sarah 6, Brian 4.
    5. Discard and draw — normal.

    Note: Playing Q♠ while behind was a poor timing choice. The power helps only when you already lead.

    House Rules (Optional)#

    Once per round, before reveal, a player may declare the House Privilege while playing a numbered card (2–10). Each suit’s privilege may be used once per round.

    • Spades — Press the Attack: If you win this trick while at Disadvantage, +1 extra.
    • Hearts — Steadfast Heart: If you lose this trick, +1.
    • Diamonds — Speculative Wager: After you place the card but before reveal, predict foe’s card as odd, even, or face. If correct, +2 after scoring.
    • Clubs — Judicious Measure: Declare it before reveal. This trick’s winner does not gain the normal base +1. All other effects still apply.

    Strategy Notes for the Four Houses#

    • Spades: Aggressive and momentum-driven. Push when ahead.
    • Hearts: Thrive on attrition. Losing well can win the war.
    • Diamonds: High variance. Lean into Advantage for explosive turns.
    • Clubs: Control tempo with locks and nullify effects.

    ♠ Spades#

    Card Summary
    A♠ Lose → Steal 1
    K♠ Win → +1; if still not ahead after scoring → -1
    Q♠ If Advantage at end of scoring → +1; if foe at Disadvantage → foe +2
    J♠ Foe played face → foe -1
    Joker♠ If win → foe chooses: you +1 or they -1

    ♥ Hearts#

    Card Summary
    A♥ Lose → +1 (foe Half Discard → +2)
    K♥ Lose → Steal 1
    Q♥ Lose → lock foe score
    J♥ Win or tie → +1
    Joker♥ +2 (win → +1 more)

    ♦ Diamonds#

    Card Summary
    A♦ Win → +1; Lose → -1; Tie → +3
    K♦ Win → Steal 1
    Q♦ Advantage pre-scoring → +1; Win → +1
    J♦ Lose → foe -1
    Joker♦ Next trick both play a random foe card

    ♣ Clubs#

    Card Summary
    A♣ Lose or tie → +1 (foe Half Deck → +2)
    K♣ Win or tie → +2; if foe has Disadvantage → foe +1
    Q♣ Win or tie → lock foe; Lose → +1
    J♣ Foe played face → nullify that power; Tie → +1
    Joker♣ Lock both scores after discarding
