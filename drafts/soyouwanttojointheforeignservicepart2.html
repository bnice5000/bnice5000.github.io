So You Want to Join the Foreign Service, Part 2

20 Feb 2016 | Updated on 04 Jun 2016 | By Brian Levin | Category Foreign Service | Tags Application
Series Part of

The Steps to join the Foreign Service

  1. Meet the requirements for the position (e.g. get my CISSP) Completed 2015-09-20
  2. Apply to the USAJobs posting Completed 2015-12-15
  3. Pass qualified evaluations panel Completed 2015-02-20
  4. Pass the oral assessment Completed 2015-05-12
  5. Get medical clearance
  6. Get security clearance
  7. Pass final suitability review panel
  8. Get placed on register
  9. Be selected off the register for an specialist class
  10. Success!

There is not much to say about the qualified evaluations panel. You receive a mysterious email that says you have passed and you will be receiving an invitation to the oral assessment. It is fairly anticlimactic. I thought there would be trombones or something, but instead just a form email.

The oral assessment panel was extremely interesting. No amount of reading about the experience would have prepared me for the process. It is just one of those things you have to experience. However, I was also a nervous potential applicant like yourself. I read everything I could find on the experience. I found two blogs particularly helpful. I greatly enjoyed the Ramble On and Technically a Diplomat blogs. Well worth a read and they probably do this topic more justice than I ever will.

However, you are reading this so you probably want my input. I will take you through it step by step and give you as much information as I can. Some details are covered by an NDA. Some details I have forgotten, but for the sake of argument we will also say they are also covered by the NDA.

I arrived the day before and stayed at a hotel less that three blocks from where I would be assessed… receiving the assessment… judged for worthiness. I ate dinner at a charming Greek restaurant. In a fit of over preparation, I walked the route from the hotel I was staying at to where I was having the oral assessment. I then did it several more times to make sure I could do it without looking at a map. After making I felt reasonably confident that I could get to the oral assessment address from my hotel. I turned in for the evening[^1].

The next morning, I got up and made my way over the building where the oral assessment would take place. My invitation set my arrival time at 9:00. I left the hotel at 8:30 to ensure that I was there in plenty of time. I arrive at 8:35. I informed the guard at the front desk of my purpose. She looked at the clock for a second and then back at me. She tilted her head and said, “Your not the earliest person for an oral assessment but you are defiantly very early.

I chuckled and tried to play it off like a timing mistake on my part. She pointed to a sign that said FSS Oral Assessment Gathering Point. There were no benches so I stood and tried to look like I was suppose to be there. About 15 minutes into my wait, a fellow applicant, herein I will call Ann, sidled up to the same sign and asked me if I was there for OMS. I said no, I was there for IMS. “Ahh a computer nerd,” she said.

We stood there in awkward silence for a while.

[1]: By turned in for the evening, I mean that I paced around the hotel room while studying my notes. I know it was counter productive and I would not gain anything from the exercise. However, sleep was illusive.
