So You Want to Join the Foreign Service, Part 1

The Steps to join the Foreign Service

  1. Meet the requirement for the position (e.g. get my CISSP) Completed 2015-09-20
  2. Apply to the USAJobs posting Completed 2015-12-15
  3. Pass qualified evaluations panel
  4. Pass the oral assessment
  5. Get medical clearance
  6. Get security clearance
  7. Pass final suitability review panel
  8. Get placed on register
  9. Be selected for an A100 class
  10. Success!

Applications Away

I just put in my application to be a Foreign Service Information Management Specialist1. I have always wanted to join the foreign service, but I was not interested in the generalist job activities. The Information Management Specialist matched my interests but you needed a degree in computer science or one of the approved certifications. For a long time I had neither. I have a degree in an applied social sciences2 and going back for a CS degree was unappealing. The shortest distance between me and my goal was to get a certification.

I had waffled on getting the certification for years. The one that made the most sense was the CISSP. My job is mostly devops and infrastructure programming. A lot of my work is tied to security. I did not need a certification for my current3 or any of my previous jobs, so it was never a priority.

This year was a bit of a roller coaster. My brother passed away and my wife is expecting our first born. I decided that life is short, it is now or never. I signed up for the CISSP exam in October and passed! Step one complete. After passing it was just a waiting game until they opened the application process. The call for applicants opened at the beginning of December and I have been working on the application ever since. I spent a good amount of time working on the essays and polishing my resume.

I just submitted my application. I am very hopeful that I will advance in the process. Fingers crossed!

  1. I am not sure I will publish this blog entry. I guess it depends on my success in the application process. So, if you are reading this, I have been hired. ↩

  2. I know what you are thinking, I wasted my youth. But, those were different times. We were so young and hopeful. ↩

  3. Bonus! My current job paid for most of my certification because it was covered under career development funds. Turns out all I had to do was ask. ↩
