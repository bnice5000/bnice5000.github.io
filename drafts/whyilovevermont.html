Why I Love Vermont

19 Jun 2016 | Updated on 06 Jul 2016 | By Brian Levin | Category Vermont | Tags VT / USA / Essay

After having lived in New York1 for most of my life, the wife and I decided that we needed a change of scenery. We both had an affinity for Vermont. We had gotten engaged in Burlington2 and had spent many vacations in the Green Mountain State. We were a big fan of all parts of the state from Burlington to Manchester, from Lake Bomoseen to Lake Fairlee.

The thought of moving to Vermont just seemed right. It seemed like the type of decision that was just meant to be. In 2012, we finally decided to pull the trigger. As my skills are more transportable, Sarah started looking for jobs in the area. Low and behold she found a perfect fit. I started the job hunt and with in a month or so, I was also gainfully employed and we took the leap and moved to the Green Mountain State.

When I say we moved, I mean we went whole hog. Sarah wanted to live the authentic Vermont experience. We bought a smallish log cabin on the side of a mountain with more property that we can use. It is nestled next to a creek. Our nearest neighbor is a quarter mile away through dense woods. The dirt road to our house is a challenge in the summer and an impediment to all but the most persistent visitors in the winter.

In the summer, there is so much to do, our cup runneth over. There is a hiking trail less that a half mile from our cabin. We hike it at least once a month. More if we can find the time. Sarah has filled our yard with gardens of the vegetable and flower variety. When it is warm and the snow has cleared from the mountain passes, Sarah and I sojourn all over the state.

Log cabins are not known for their insulation. I have done much to insulate and abate air penetration but there is only so much to be done. The best way to keep the log cabin warm is to produce heat. Our main source of heat is wood. The woodstove uses at a minimum six cords of wood in the house warm even in the warmest of winters. Eight cords for a standard Vermont winter has been our average and ten one particularly cold winter. Be it shoveling snow or bringing in wood, winter tends to be a time for quit contemplation.

I know for some of you that does not sound like a great place to live. I think its rural charm could win almost anyone over. The quiet spirit of Vermont is the reason that we enjoy being here so much. It is the reason that we are glad we moved here.

  1. Upstate and Western New York. Never the City (not that there is anything wrong with it, just not my cup of tea). Also, there were brief sojourns to Massachusetts and Connecticut. All four places are topics for other essays. ↩

  2. I proposed at the riverfront, but we stayed at the Howard Johnson Inn. Alas, the HoJo’s Inn is no more. ↩
